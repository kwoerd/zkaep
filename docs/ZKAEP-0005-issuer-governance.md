# ZKAEP-0005: Issuer Governance

Status: **Draft**  
Version: **0.1**

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the governance expectations for ZK-AEP credential issuers.

ZK-AEP depends on trustworthy issuance. A privacy-preserving proof is only as reliable as the credential and issuer process behind it.

---

## 2. Approved Issuers

Approved issuers may include, depending on the eligibility policy and legal context:

- licensed medical providers;
- clinics;
- laboratories;
- approved administrative reviewers;
- independent credentialing organizations;
- sport-specific eligibility panels;
- bioethics-reviewed verification partners;
- other governance-approved issuers.

---

## 3. Issuer Approval Criteria

Issuer approval SHOULD consider:

- competence;
- neutrality;
- accuracy;
- privacy practices;
- consent procedures;
- conflict of interest;
- review or appeal cooperation;
- legal and regulatory compliance;
- data minimization;
- ability to support revocation or correction.

---

## 4. No Automatic Trust

No issuer should be treated as automatically trusted merely because it is technical, medical, decentralized, popular, or affiliated with the ecosystem.

Issuer authority must be granted through a published governance process.

---

## 5. Issuer Removal

A governing authority SHOULD be able to remove or suspend issuers for:

- inaccuracy;
- bias;
- misconduct;
- security compromise;
- privacy violations;
- failure to follow policy;
- failure to support appeals or corrections;
- loss of legal or professional standing.

---

## 6. Participant Rights

Issuer governance should preserve participant rights, including:

- informed consent;
- access to correction processes;
- explanation of what is being verified;
- appeal or exception path;
- data minimization;
- protection from unnecessary disclosure.
